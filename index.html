Our purpose

Our purpose, as the official dog breed informative website is to inform as many people as public to the intricacies and relevant information they may wish to know about dog breeds. We aim to give our users relevant information to make the best possible decision they can for which dog breed will be best for them to adopt using the the information we provide about individual dog breeds. I hope to help people make the most confident decision on which breed of dog they will decide to adopt and have customers feel safe in looking for an individual dog breed type with our information provided about that dog. I also hope this information is valuable to people just curious about dog breeds, not necessarily looking to adopt a dog breed. I have researched these dog breeds thoroughly using relevant sources and aim to give the best, most accurate and relevant dog breed information possible to all users to aid you in finding the dog of your dream dog.

Why you should adopt a dog

Every family should have a pet, a pet can be a key part in your family structure and are scientifically proven to increase dopamine levels in brains of owners significantly. Dogs are one of the most ideal pets to get, they are, for the most part, adaptive to you, or your family's lifestyle. Dogs, particularly if you have a breed adapted to your lifestyle, can be a good pet for you even if you live alone, go to work for ten hours a day, have a small living space, all based on the breed of dog you own. A Pembroke Welsh Corgi could be perfect for you if you have a small living space, a great dane might better suit you if you have a larger living space and a larger lawn where they can be more active. Dogs require attention and love, but this is a great thing for people to do and really helps build people to be better people. Adopting a dog from a shelter or otherwise is much better than buying from a breeder for everyone involved. It is often thought that dogs that are at adoption shelters are less cleanly, possibly more dangerous and could have unknown problems as their parents and breed may not be certainly known, however this is usually, for the most part, covered by the vetenarians and workers at animal shelters and doesn't usually cause much issues. Furthermore, adopting a dog is better for you and the animal industry. It could get a dog that may have been planned to be put down or a dog that would be locked up in a kennel for months or even years into a safe home with you. It will also cost you much less than buying a dog from a breeder if you adopt that dog. The regular adoption fee for dogs from an animal shelter usually ranges from about $100 to $250, however from a breeder can cost anywhere from $1000 into the multiple thousands. Adopting a dog is the best way to help bring light into an animals life and improve your own life.
